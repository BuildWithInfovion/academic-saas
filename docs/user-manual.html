🏫 Platform Overview

The Academic SaaS Platform is a cloud-based school management system that brings together all aspects of school operations — student records, attendance, examinations, fees, timetables, staff management, and more — into a single, unified platform.

The platform is accessed through a web browser. There is no software to install. Each school is given a unique Institution Code (also called School Code) that identifies your school on the platform.

Multi-Portal System: The platform has separate portals for each role (Operator, Teacher, Principal, Director, Accountant, Receptionist, Parent, Student). Each portal shows only the information and tools relevant to that role.

🚀 Getting Started

Logging In

All users — staff, parents, and students — log in through the same URL. The system automatically routes you to your correct portal based on your role.

  1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari) and go to the school's platform URL provided by your school administrator.
  2. On the login page, enter your Institution Code (provided by the school). This is a short code that identifies your school (e.g., STJOSEPH2024).
  3. Enter your Email address or 10-digit phone number registered with the school.
  4. Enter your Password and click Sign In.
  5. If you have more than one role assigned (e.g., both Teacher and Class Teacher), you will be taken directly to the appropriate portal. The system selects the correct portal automatically.
Important: The Institution Code is mandatory. Without it, the system cannot identify which school you belong to. Contact your school administrator if you do not have this code.

First Login & Changing Your Password

  1. On your first login, you will receive a temporary password from your school administrator. Use it to log in.
  2. After logging in, it is strongly recommended to change your password immediately. Go to your Profile / Settings page.
  3. Enter your current (temporary) password, then enter and confirm your new password.
  4. Click Save Changes. Your new password takes effect immediately.
Forgot Password? On the login page, click "Forgot Password?". Enter your registered email address and you will receive a password reset link. If you registered with only a phone number (no email), contact your school administrator to reset your password.

Session & Security

  • Your session stays active for a period of time. If you are inactive for too long, you will be logged out automatically.
  • Do not share your password with anyone, including school administrators.
  • Always click Logout when using a shared or public computer.
  • The platform works best on modern browsers. Keep your browser updated.

Operator Dashboard

Staff Management

Manage all staff accounts: create new users, assign roles, view assignments, and reset passwords.

Adding a New Staff Member

  1. Go to Dashboard → Staff. The staff list page opens.
  2. Click + Add Staff in the top-right corner.
  3. Enter the staff member's Email address (e.g., rajesh@school.com). If they do not have an email, enter their 10-digit mobile number instead.
  4. Set a password. You can click Generate to auto-create a strong random password, or type one manually.
  5. Select a Role from the dropdown (Teacher, Accountant, Receptionist, etc.).
  6. Click Create Account. A confirmation screen shows the credentials — note them down and share with the staff member. The password is not shown again.
Password Visibility: The generated password is only shown once at creation time. Make sure to copy it and share it securely with the staff member.

Viewing a Staff Member's Profile

  1. On the Staff page, click any row in the table to open the staff member's profile panel on the right side.
  2. The panel shows: assigned roles, the class(es) they are class teacher of, and subjects they teach.
  3. To assign an additional role, select it from the Assign Additional Role dropdown and click Add.

Resetting a Staff Member's Password

  1. Open the staff member's profile panel by clicking their row.
  2. Click Reset Password at the bottom of the panel.
  3. Confirm when prompted. A new random password is generated and displayed immediately.
  4. Share the new password with the staff member and ask them to change it after logging in.

Deleting a Staff Account

  1. Open the staff member's profile panel, or find the Delete button on their row in the table.
  2. Click Delete User and confirm. This action is permanent.
Warning: Deleting a staff account is irreversible. All associated data such as attendance records and class assignments will be unlinked. Only delete accounts for staff who have permanently left the school.

Student Management

Manage the full student lifecycle: enrolment, profile updates, parent linking, and exit.

Adding a New Student

  1. Go to Dashboard → Students.
  2. Click + Enrol Student.
  3. Fill in the student's details: First Name, Last Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Admission Number (must be unique), Roll Number (optional), and the Class/Section they are joining.
  4. Click Save. The student is enrolled and appears in the class list.

Linking a Parent to a Student

Parents need a parent portal account. First create the parent account, then link it to the student.

  1. Create the parent's account via Staff → Add Staff with the role Parent.
  2. Go to Students → [Student Name] to open the student's detail page.
  3. In the Parent/Guardian section, click Link Parent.
  4. Search for the parent by email or phone and select them. Click Confirm Link.
  5. The parent can now see this child's data in their portal.

Student Directory

  1. Go to Dashboard → Students → Directory for a searchable, filterable view of all students across all classes.
  2. Use the search bar to find students by name or admission number.
  3. Use the class filter to narrow to a specific class or section.

Classes & Subjects Setup

The academic structure (classes, sections, and subjects) must be set up before any other features can be used.

Setting Up Classes

  1. Go to Dashboard → Classes.
  2. Click + Add Class to create a root class (e.g., "Class 1", "Class 2").
  3. To add sections under a class (e.g., "A", "B"), click the class name to expand it, then click + Add Section.
  4. Set a Display Name for the section (e.g., "1-A", "Class 1 – A"). This is shown throughout the system.

Assigning a Class Teacher

  1. On the Classes page, click on a class/section row.
  2. In the detail panel, click Set Class Teacher.
  3. Select the teacher from the dropdown (shows all users with the Teacher role).
  4. Click Save. The teacher now appears as Class Teacher and can mark attendance and manage promotions for that class.

Setting Up Subjects

  1. Go to Dashboard → Subjects.
  2. Click + Add Subject. Enter the subject Name (e.g., "Mathematics") and an optional Code (e.g., "MATH").
  3. To assign a subject to a class and a teacher, click the subject row, then use Assign to Class and select the class and the teaching teacher.

Timetable

Build the weekly timetable for all classes. Define period timings, configure breaks, and assign cover duties.

Configuring Period Timings

  1. Go to Dashboard → Timetable.
  2. Click Period Configuration or the settings gear icon.
  3. Add each time block of the school day: enter a Label (e.g., "Period 1"), Start Time, End Time, and whether it is a Break (e.g., Lunch Break, Short Break).
  4. Drag to reorder entries if needed. Click Save.

Building the Timetable for a Class

  1. On the Timetable page, select the Class/Section from the dropdown.
  2. The weekly grid is displayed with columns for each day (Monday–Saturday) and rows for each period.
  3. Click any cell to assign a subject. Select the Subject from the dropdown.
  4. The teacher assigned to that subject is automatically associated. Click Save.
  5. Repeat for all periods and all days.

Cover Duties (Substitute Teachers)

  1. Go to Dashboard → Timetable → Covers.
  2. Click + Assign Cover.
  3. Select the Date, the Absent Teacher, the Period, the Class, and the Covering Teacher.
  4. Click Save. The covering teacher will see this duty highlighted on their timetable page for that day.

Attendance

Operators can view and override attendance for any class on any date.

Viewing Class Attendance

  1. Go to Dashboard → Attendance.
  2. Select the Class/Section from the dropdown.
  3. Select the Date (defaults to today).
  4. The list of students appears with their current attendance status (Present / Absent / Late / Leave).

Marking or Correcting Attendance

  1. On the Attendance page with the class and date selected, click the status badge next to any student to cycle through: P Present → A Absent → L Late → Lv Leave.
  2. Use All Present or All Absent buttons for bulk marking.
  3. Click Save Attendance to commit the changes.

Examinations

Create and manage examinations, assign subjects to teachers, and control result visibility.

Creating an Examination

  1. Go to Dashboard → Exams.
  2. Click + New Exam.
  3. Enter the Exam Name (e.g., "Term 1 Examination"), select the Academic Year, and optionally set start/end dates, exam centre, and reporting time.
  4. Click Create. The exam is created in Draft status.

Adding Subjects to an Exam

  1. Open the exam by clicking its name.
  2. Click + Add Subject.
  3. Select the Class/Section, the Subject, set Maximum Marks and Passing Marks.
  4. Optionally set an Exam Date and Time for the admit card schedule.
  5. Click Add. Repeat for all subjects and classes.
Bulk Add: Use Bulk Add Subjects to add multiple subjects at once for a class, or Clone Subjects to copy the subject structure from another exam.

Activating an Exam (Opening for Mark Entry)

  1. Open the exam.
  2. Click Change Status → Active.
  3. Teachers assigned to those subjects can now enter marks from their portal.

Completing an Exam (Publishing Results)

  1. Once all marks are entered, open the exam.
  2. Click Change Status → Completed.
  3. Results are now visible to parents and students in their portals.
Important: Parents can only see results for exams that are marked Completed. Exams in Active or Draft status are not visible to parents.

Fees

Set up the fee structure for the school, record payments, and monitor outstanding dues.

Step 1 — Create Fee Heads

  1. Go to Dashboard → Fees.
  2. Click + Add Fee Head.
  3. Enter the fee head name (e.g., "Tuition Fee", "Library Fee", "Transport Fee").
  4. Click Save. Repeat for all types of fees your school collects.

Step 2 — Create Fee Structures (Assign amounts to classes)

  1. On the Fees page, go to Fee Structures.
  2. Select the Academic Year and Class/Section.
  3. Click + Add Structure.
  4. Select the Fee Head, enter the Total Amount for the year.
  5. Optionally, split it into installments by setting installment names (e.g., "Q1 April", "Q2 July") and due dates.
  6. Click Save.

Recording a Fee Payment

  1. On the Fees page, go to Collect Fees.
  2. Search for the student by name or admission number. Click the student from the results.
  3. The student's outstanding balance and fee breakdown are shown.
  4. Click + Record Payment.
  5. Select the Fee Head, enter the Amount, select the Payment Mode (Cash, UPI, Cheque, Bank Transfer, DD), and add any remarks.
  6. Click Save Payment. A receipt is generated automatically.
  7. Click Print next to the payment to print the official receipt.

Viewing Fee Defaulters

  1. On the Fees page, go to the Defaulters tab.
  2. Select the Academic Year and optionally a Class to filter.
  3. All students with an outstanding balance are listed with the amounts due.

Salary

Record and manage staff salary disbursements.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Salary.
  2. Select the staff member and the month/year.
  3. Enter the basic salary, allowances, and deductions as applicable.
  4. Click Save. The salary record is stored for future reference and reporting.

Transfer Certificates (TC)

Issue Transfer Certificates to students who are leaving the school.

Generating a New TC

  1. Go to Dashboard → Transfer Certificates.
  2. Click + New TC.
  3. Search for and select the student.
  4. Fill in the TC details: date of leaving, reason, conduct, and any remarks.
  5. Click Generate TC. The TC is saved and can be printed.

Admission Inquiries

Track prospective student inquiries from initial contact through to enrollment.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Inquiries.
  2. All logged inquiries are listed with their current status: New → Contacted → Visited → Enrolled or Dropped.
  3. To log a new inquiry, click + New Inquiry, fill in the parent's name, phone, email, and the class of interest, then click Save.
  4. To update status: click Edit on any inquiry, change the status, and save.

Announcements

Post notices visible to specific roles or the entire school.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Announcements.
  2. Click + New Announcement.
  3. Enter the Title and Content of the announcement.
  4. Select the Target Audience: All Staff, All Teachers, Parents, Students, or specific roles.
  5. Set an optional Expiry Date after which the announcement is hidden.
  6. Click Publish. The announcement appears immediately in the selected portals.

Academic Calendar

Manage school events, holidays, and important dates visible to all users.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Calendar.
  2. Click any date to add an event on that day.
  3. Enter the Event Name, select the Type (Holiday, Exam, Event, etc.), and add a description.
  4. Click Save. The event appears on everyone's calendar across all portals.

Promote Students

Bulk-move students to the next class at the end of the academic year.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Promote.
  2. Select the Action: Promote (move to next class), Transfer (move to another class), or Hold Back (keep in current class).
  3. Select the Source Class (current class) and the Target Class (new class, if applicable).
  4. The student list appears. All students are selected by default. Deselect any students to exclude them.
  5. Click Confirm & Apply. The selected students are moved to their new class.
One-Way Operation: Promotion cannot be automatically reversed. Verify your selections carefully before confirming.

Settings

Configure school information and academic year settings.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Settings.
  2. School Information: Update the school name, board affiliation, address, phone, and email. This information appears on printed receipts, report cards, and admit cards.
  3. Academic Year: Create a new academic year (e.g., "2026-27"), set its start and end dates, and mark it as the current year.
  4. Click Save after each section.

📚 Teacher Portal

Teacher Portal

For teaching staff. Mark student attendance, enter exam marks, view your timetable, and manage your own attendance.
Dashboard Mark Attendance Enter Marks My Timetable Promote Students My Attendance Announcements Calendar

Teacher Dashboard (Overview)

The dashboard shows a summary of your classes and quick action buttons.

  • If you are a Class Teacher, your assigned class(es) are shown with a coloured banner, including the total number of boys and girls.
  • Quick action buttons let you jump directly to Mark Attendance or Enter Marks.
  • If you teach subjects in other classes (but are not their class teacher), those classes appear under Subject Teaching.

Marking Student Attendance

  1. Click Mark Attendance from the dashboard or the sidebar.
  2. Select the Class/Section from the dropdown. Only your assigned classes are shown.
  3. The Date defaults to today. Change it if marking attendance for a different day.
  4. The student list loads automatically. By default, all students are set to Present.
  5. Click the status badge next to any student to cycle: P Present → A Absent → L Late → Lv Leave.
  6. Use All Present or All Absent buttons for bulk marking.
  7. The summary bar at the top shows a live count of each status.
  8. When done, click Save Attendance.
Re-opening: If you have already submitted attendance for a date and open it again, the saved values are pre-loaded. You can correct them and save again.

Entering Exam Marks

  1. Click Enter Marks from the dashboard or sidebar.
  2. Select the Examination from the dropdown. Only exams with active status that include your assigned subjects are shown.
  3. Below the exam selector, buttons show each combination of Class + Subject assigned to you. Click one to select it.
  4. The student list loads with any previously saved marks pre-filled.
  5. Enter marks in the Marks column for each student. The maximum marks are shown in the column header.
  6. If a student was absent for the exam, tick the Absent checkbox. The marks field is disabled for absent students.
  7. A red border appears if entered marks exceed the maximum — you must correct this before saving.
  8. The progress indicator (e.g., "18/35 entered") shows how many students have marks entered.
  9. Click Save Marks when done.

Viewing My Timetable

  1. Click My Timetable in the sidebar.
  2. Use the Day View or Week View toggle at the top right.
  3. In Day View, click any day tab to see your periods for that day. Period numbers and time slots are shown.
  4. In Week View, a full grid of all days and periods is displayed.
  5. If you have been assigned as a Cover Teacher for the current day, a yellow banner at the top lists the extra periods you need to cover, the class, and the absent teacher's name.

Promoting Students (Class Teachers Only)

This feature is only available to teachers who are assigned as Class Teacher of at least one class.

  1. Click Promote Students in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Action: Promote, Transfer, or Hold Back.
  3. Select the From Class (one of your classes) and the To Class (any class in the school).
  4. The student list for your class loads. All students are selected by default — deselect any you want to skip.
  5. Click Apply to N Students or Confirm & Apply at the bottom.

My Attendance (Self-Marking)

  1. Click My Attendance in the sidebar.
  2. The Today's card at the top shows whether you have marked attendance today.
  3. If not yet marked: select your status (Present, Late, Half Day), optionally add a note, and click Mark.
  4. If already marked and you need to clock out, click the Clock Out button.
  5. The Monthly Records tab shows all your attendance entries for the current month.
  6. The Leave Requests tab lets you apply for leave: click + Apply for Leave, enter start date, end date, and reason, then click Submit Request.
  7. You can track the status of leave requests (Pending / Approved / Rejected) from the same tab.

🎓 Principal Portal

Principal Portal

School Principal's oversight dashboard. Monitor attendance, fees, staff leave, and academic progress — without modifying core data.
Overview Attendance Reports Fee Reports Timetable Staff Attendance Announcements Calendar Exams Settings

Overview Dashboard

The Principal's dashboard shows at a glance:

  • Total Students with boys/girls breakdown
  • Current Academic Year
  • Total Classes
  • Staff Accounts count
  • Attendance Defaulters (below 75% this month) — top 8 across sampled classes
  • Fee Defaulters — students with outstanding balances (top 8)

Attendance Reports

  1. Click Attendance in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Class/Section and Month.
  3. Adjust the Threshold (default: 75%) to set what counts as a defaulter.
  4. The page shows: total students, number of defaulters, and a full table of all students with their attendance percentage.
  5. Students below the threshold are highlighted in red with a "Defaulter" badge.

Fee Reports

  1. Click Fees in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Academic Year and optionally filter by Class.
  3. Defaulters tab: Lists all students with outstanding fee balances, showing total due, amount paid, and balance.
  4. Today's Collection tab: Shows all fee payments recorded today with receipt numbers, student names, and amounts.

Staff Attendance & Leave Management

  1. Click Staff Attendance in the sidebar.
  2. Three tabs are available:
    • Leave Requests: All pending/approved/rejected leave requests from all staff. The tab badge shows the number of pending requests.
    • Daily View: Select a date to see which staff members marked attendance and their clock-in/out times.
    • Monthly Report: Select month and year to see a summary of each staff member's present, late, half-day, and absent days.
  3. To approve or reject a leave request, click the Approve or Reject button on any pending request row.

📊 Director Portal

Director Portal

For school directors, trustees, or management. High-level oversight with the ability to manage staff accounts.
Institution Overview Reports Staff Management Settings

Institution Overview

The Director's homepage shows high-level metrics:

  • Total students, staff accounts, classes, and current academic year
  • Attendance Alerts: Number of students below 75% attendance this month
  • Fee Defaulters: Total number of students with outstanding balances
  • Active Examinations: Exams currently in progress
  • Quick navigation cards to Reports, Staff Directory, and Settings

Reports

Three tabbed reports are available:

  • Attendance tab: Select a class to see all students below 75% attendance for the current month.
  • Fees tab: Full list of fee defaulters for the current academic year with balances.
  • Examinations tab: All exams for the current year with their status (Draft / Active / Completed).

Staff Management

The Director has full staff management capability — identical to the Operator's staff page.

  1. Click Staff in the sidebar.
  2. View all staff members, filter by role, add new staff, view profiles, assign roles, and reset passwords.
  3. See the Operator → Staff Management section for detailed instructions.

💰 Accountant Portal

Accountant Portal

For accounts and finance staff. Record fee collections, print receipts, and view payment summaries.
Overview Fee Collection My Attendance Announcements

Overview

The accountant's homepage shows four key financial metrics:

  • Today's Collection: Total fee amount collected today
  • This Month: Total collected in the current month
  • Total Due: Total outstanding balance across all students
  • Total Students: Total enrolled students

Fee Collection

  1. Click Fee Collection in the sidebar or the quick action button.
  2. In the Search Student box, type the student's name or admission number, then click Search (or press Enter).
  3. A dropdown list of matching students appears. Click the correct student to select them.
  4. The student's balance summary is shown: Total Due, Total Paid, and Outstanding balance.
  5. The fee breakdown table shows each fee head with how much is due, paid, and remaining.
  6. Click + Record Payment.
  7. Select the Fee Head, enter the Amount, choose the Payment Mode, and add optional remarks.
  8. Click Save Payment. The payment is recorded and the balance updates immediately.
  9. To print the receipt, find the payment in the Payment History table and click Print.
Receipt Printing: A print dialog opens in a new browser window. Click Print to send to your printer. The receipt includes school name, student details, fee head, amount, payment mode, and receipt number.

📋 Receptionist Portal

Receptionist Portal

For front-office staff. Log admission inquiries from walk-ins and phone calls, track follow-ups through to enrollment.
Admission Inquiries Announcements

Managing Admission Inquiries

The main page of the receptionist portal is the Inquiry Management screen. All inquiries are listed here with their current status.

Logging a New Inquiry

  1. Click + New Inquiry in the top-right corner.
  2. Fill in the parent/student details:
    • First Name (required)
    • Last Name
    • Phone (required)
    • Email (optional)
    • Class Interest (e.g., "Class 3", "Nursery")
    • Notes (any additional details)
  3. Click Log Inquiry. The inquiry is saved with status New.

Updating Inquiry Status

The status column in each row has a dropdown. Change it directly:

StatusMeaning
NewInquiry just received, not yet followed up
ContactedInitial contact made with the family
VisitedFamily has visited the school
EnrolledStudent has been admitted
DroppedNo longer interested

Filtering Inquiries

  1. Click any status card at the top (New, Contacted, Visited, Enrolled, Dropped) to filter the table to only that status. Click again to remove the filter.
  2. Alternatively, use the pill filter buttons below the stats cards.

Editing an Inquiry

  1. Click Edit on any inquiry row.
  2. The form opens pre-filled. Update any details and click Update.

🏢 Non-Teaching Staff Portal

Non-Teaching Staff Portal

For support staff (office staff, peons, librarians, etc.). Mark daily attendance and view school announcements.
Overview My Attendance Announcements

Using the Non-Teaching Staff Portal

The portal has two main functions: self-attendance and announcements.

Marking Your Attendance

  1. Click My Attendance from the overview page or sidebar.
  2. The current date is displayed. If you have not marked today, select your status: Present, Late, or Half Day.
  3. Optionally add a note (e.g., "Working from home").
  4. Click Mark.
  5. Once marked, you can Clock Out by clicking the Clock Out button.

Applying for Leave

  1. On the My Attendance page, click the Leave Requests tab.
  2. Click + Apply for Leave.
  3. Enter the From Date, To Date, and Reason.
  4. Click Submit Request. Your leave request is sent to the Principal for approval.
  5. Check back on this tab to see if it has been Approved or Rejected.

👨‍👩‍👧 Parent Portal

Parent Portal

For parents and guardians. Monitor your child's attendance, marks, fees, timetable, and school announcements.
Overview Attendance Marks & Results Exams Fee Status Timetable Announcements Calendar
Before You Start: Your child's record must be linked to your account by the school administrator. If you see a message saying "Your child's record has not been linked yet", please contact the school office.

Parent Overview Dashboard

The homepage gives you an instant status of your child(ren) today:

  • If your child was marked absent today, a red alert banner appears at the top with your child's name.
  • If your child is present (and attendance was taken), a green banner confirms this.
  • Your linked children are shown as cards with their class name.
  • Quick link buttons take you to Attendance, Marks & Results, Fee Status, and Announcements.

Checking Child's Attendance

  1. Click Attendance from the dashboard or sidebar.
  2. If you have multiple children, select the child from the Child dropdown.
  3. Select the Month to view.
  4. A summary shows: Present, Absent, Late, and overall Attendance %.
  5. If attendance drops below 75%, a red warning is shown — please contact the class teacher.
  6. The day-by-day record is listed below the summary.

Viewing Marks & Results

  1. Click Marks & Results in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Child (if multiple), Academic Year, and Examination.
  3. Only Completed exams are shown (results published by the school).
  4. The scorecard shows: marks obtained, maximum marks, percentage, grade, pass/fail status for each subject, and the overall class rank.

Exam Schedule & Admit Cards

  1. Click Exams in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Child and Academic Year.
  3. All exams are listed with their status: In Progress, Completed, or Upcoming.
  4. For upcoming or active exams: click Admit Card to download and print the hall ticket. The admit card includes the exam schedule (subject-wise dates and times).
  5. For completed exams: click View Results to see the scorecard. Click Print Report Card to print an official report card.
Printing: The report card and admit card open in a print-ready format in a new window. Your browser's print dialog will open automatically. Use Ctrl+P (Windows) or Cmd+P (Mac) if it does not open automatically.

Fee Status

  1. Click Fee Status in the sidebar.
  2. Select the Child (if multiple).
  3. At the top, any upcoming or overdue fee dues are highlighted in a panel with the amount and due date. Overdue dues are shown in red.
  4. The balance summary shows: Total Due, Total Paid, and Outstanding Balance.
  5. The Fee Breakdown table shows dues and payments per fee head.
  6. The Payment History table lists all receipts. Click Print to reprint any receipt.

Class Timetable

  1. Click Timetable in the sidebar.
  2. Select the child (if multiple). The weekly timetable for your child's class is displayed as a grid.
  3. Each cell shows the subject name and teacher name for that period.
  4. Break periods (Lunch, Short Break) are shown as coloured rows spanning all days.

🎒 Student Portal

Student Portal

For students. Currently shows a welcome screen. Full features (timetable, attendance, marks) are coming soon.
Overview
Student Portal Status: The student portal is currently in its initial phase. It shows a welcome message confirming your account is set up. Full self-service features (timetable, attendance summary, exam results, fee status) are being actively developed and will be available in the next release. Until then, parents can access all this information through the Parent Portal.

🔐 Roles & Permissions

Each user account is assigned one or more roles. Roles determine which portal is accessed and what actions are permitted.

Role Portal Can Read Can Write Restrictions
Super Admin / Admin Operator Dashboard Everything Everything None
Principal Principal Portal Students, staff, attendance, fees, exams Approve/reject leave requests Cannot create/delete students or staff
Director Director Portal Everything Staff accounts, settings Cannot modify student/fee data directly
Teacher Teacher Portal Own classes & subjects only Attendance (own classes), exam marks (own subjects) Cannot see other teachers' classes
Accountant Accountant Portal Fees, students Record fee payments Cannot modify fee structures
Receptionist Receptionist Portal Inquiries, announcements Log and update inquiries No access to student records or fees
Non-Teaching Staff Non-Teaching Staff Portal Announcements Own attendance, leave requests No access to students, fees, or exams
Parent Parent Portal Own children only None Can only see their own linked children
Student Student Portal Own data (coming soon) None Limited access in current version
Multiple Roles: A user can have more than one role (e.g., a teacher who is also the principal). In this case, they can access both portals. The system routes them based on which role is primary.

📖 Glossary

TermDefinition
Institution CodeA unique short code assigned to each school on the platform. Required at login to identify your school. Also called School Code.
Academic UnitA class or section (e.g., "Class 5 – A"). The system supports two levels: Class → Section.
Leaf UnitThe lowest-level academic unit (typically a section). Students are enrolled directly in leaf units.
Class TeacherA teacher designated as the primary teacher for a specific class/section. They can mark attendance, promote students, and access full class records.
Fee HeadA category of fee (e.g., Tuition Fee, Library Fee, Transport Fee). Schools can define as many as needed.
Fee StructureThe configuration that assigns specific fee amounts (and installments) to a class for a given academic year.
InstallmentA scheduled partial payment within a fee structure (e.g., Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4). Each installment has a due date.
DefaulterA student who has outstanding fee dues (fee defaulter) or whose attendance percentage falls below the set threshold (attendance defaulter).
Receipt NoA unique auto-generated number for each fee payment. Used to identify and print receipts.
Cover DutyWhen a teacher is absent, another teacher is assigned to cover their classes. The covering teacher sees this as a "Cover Duty" on their timetable.
Admit Card / Hall TicketAn official document given to students before exams. It lists their exam schedule (subject, date, time) and must be presented at the exam hall.
ScorecardA detailed result sheet showing a student's marks in each subject, percentage, grade, and class rank for an examination.
Report CardA printable document (same data as scorecard) in official school letterhead format, including signature lines for Class Teacher, Parent, and Principal.
Transfer Certificate (TC)An official document issued when a student leaves the school, confirming their enrollment and conduct.
Academic YearThe school year period (e.g., 2025-26). All data — fees, exams, timetables — is organised under an academic year.
PromoteMoving students from their current class to the next class at the end of the academic year.
Hold BackKeeping a student in the same class for another year (not promoting them).
Clock In / Clock OutStaff self-attendance feature. Clock In is recorded automatically when you mark attendance. Clock Out is done manually at the end of the day.

❓ FAQ & Troubleshooting

❓ I cannot log in — it says "Invalid credentials"

Check the following:

  • The Institution Code is entered correctly (case-sensitive). Ask your administrator if unsure.
  • Your email or phone number is exactly as registered with the school.
  • Caps Lock is not accidentally on.
  • If you recently had your password reset, use the new password provided.
  • If the problem persists, click Forgot Password or contact your school administrator.

❓ I am a teacher but I cannot see any classes in the attendance page

Your classes have not been assigned yet. Ask your school administrator to:

  • Assign you as Class Teacher for a class (for class attendance).
  • Assign you as a Subject Teacher in the Subjects page (for subject-specific attendance).

❓ I am a teacher but I cannot see any exams in the Mark Entry page

Two conditions must be met for an exam to appear:

  • The exam must have a status of Active (not Draft or Completed).
  • You must be assigned as the subject teacher for at least one subject in that exam. Ask the operator to assign your subjects.

❓ As a parent, I can log in but I cannot see my child's data

Your account needs to be linked to your child's student record. This is done by the school administrator. Contact the school office and provide your registered email/phone number and your child's admission number. Once linked, refresh the page and the data will appear.

❓ The parent cannot see exam results even though marks have been entered

Results are visible to parents only when the exam status is Completed. The school operator must change the exam status from Active to Completed after all marks have been entered. Ask your administrator to complete the exam.

❓ The Print button does not open the print dialog

The system opens a new browser window/tab for printing. Check that your browser is not blocking pop-ups:

  • In Chrome: click the pop-up blocked icon in the address bar and choose "Always allow pop-ups from this site".
  • In Firefox: click the notification bar and allow pop-ups for this site.
  • In Safari: go to Safari → Preferences → Websites → Pop-Up Windows and allow for this site.

❓ Attendance was saved but the wrong status was recorded for a student

Attendance can be corrected at any time. Open the attendance page, select the same class and date, change the student's status to the correct value, and click Save Attendance again. The record is updated.

❓ I submitted a leave request but my principal has not seen it

Leave requests appear in the Principal's Staff Attendance → Leave Requests tab. The badge on the tab shows how many pending requests there are. Please ask your principal to review the pending requests in their portal.

❓ I accidentally promoted a student to the wrong class

Contact the school administrator (Operator). The operator can manually transfer the student back by using the Promote page with the Transfer action — selecting the incorrect class as source and the correct class as target.

❓ The platform shows a "Session expired" message

Your login session has timed out due to inactivity. Click the link to go to the login page and sign in again. Your data is safe — nothing was lost.

❓ How do I change my password after logging in?

Go to your profile settings (usually accessible from the top-right user menu). Look for Change Password or Security Settings. Enter your current password and your new password, then save.
